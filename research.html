Research

Conducting research on relevant project proposal engines

Research Introduction

In order to fully comprehend the functionalities that this CFP Portal Engine will be required to have, extensive research had to be undertaken. This included looking at current platforms that provide a similar or relevant service in order to provide an idea on the structure of their systems, functionalities and databases.

Once a set number of platforms have been identified, the next steps were to make a note of each platform, categorising the functionalities and overall design of their system, looking at the pros and cons, hence providing us with more information on how we would like to build our project proposal engine.

It is worth noting that current project proposal engines offer a design editor that allows its users to graphically represent their proposal in an artistic and attractive manner, however, in our implementation, due to the simplistic and easy-to-use user requirement, we decided that it was better for data collection to be entirely handled by forms.


Related Platform - Reflections & Opinions


After having done comprehensive research, we will be showcasing below the steps and the process we undertook while presenting the most important features we have explored within other project proposal platforms. Our research is outlined below.


Better Proposals

Better proposals is a paid web application with a mobile application option that focuses on simplifying the process of proposing projects to potential clients. Their focus is on providing businesses a very attractive and easy method to propose projects and in turn, getting them signed and approved. This is done through their in built "Automatic Designer" application which makes it easy to create good-looking proposals in a matter of minutes. [1]

The link to their website: Better Proposals

Fig. Better Proposals Example Dashboard

Features
  • "Automatic Designer" page allows users to easily create attractive proposals in minutes
  • A multitude of templates are provided, ranging from a variety of themes, that look great on desktop, tablet and mobile devices
  • Seamless user interface that focuses on ease of use and access
  • Clients can immediately sign your proposal and pay your fees, all from the secure and encrypted weblink that you send them
  • A user will be informed when their clients read your proposals, giving you consistent updates
  • Reporting and tracking features allow users to monitor the status of their proposals, allowing them to track who's ready for a follow up and who's ready to sign

Bidsketch

This proposal management software boasts a very large template library that focuses on providing a professional feel to its users. It is implemented as a web application with no mobile option, unlike Better Proposals. Their platform markets itself as an all in one commercial software that allows business to submit professional looking proposals to their clients. Amongst most features, a notable one worth mentioning is the option to modify and work with fees, allowing their users full control of the payment. In addition to this, the next steps after a proposal has been approved can be automated, where the web application can be used to handle messages, deposits, emails and notifications. [2]

The link to their website: Bidsketch

Fig. Bidsketch Example Dashboard

Features
  • Provides a large template library for users to cut proposal time in half
  • Allows users to include optional fees in their proposal document in order to increase revenue
  • Provides clients with a professional landing page where they can view, approve, export and comment on their proposal
  • Next steps of approved proposals can be chosen to be automated, allowing users to be notified automatically of any progress
  • Users are immediately notified of the status of their proposal and given data on their clients, allowing users to improve close rates
  • All proposals can be signed off electronically and instantly so that the sales process is expedited quickly

Qwilr

Qwilr is centred around a business approach of ensuring its users and clients are happy with the nature, feel and look of their proposals. Their web application includes an extremely easy and simple design interface, which allows users of all experience levels to curate interactive, attractive and mobile-responsive documents. Dynamic and embedded content are an additional selling point, where users can integrate videos, calendars, and even Google Maps into their proposals. [3]

The link to their website: Qwilr

Fig. Qwilr Example Dashboard
Features
  • Provides a designer editor which easily allows users to create polished documents using modular design blocks
  • Interactive quoting allows clients to choose the pricing package that fits their needs
  • Robust payment system provides clients with the ability to accept, e-sign and pay in one flow, from any device
  • Page notifications immediately tells you when a document is viewed or accepted
  • Analytics provide insight into how and when prospective clients are interacting with your documents
  • Seamless integration with leading CRM, sales and accounting software

EU Calls

This is a European Platform for sending, collecting and reviewing project proposals. The participants have to find a call/topic, find partners and plan their work and budget. This project came useful when we were researching their way of marking and evaluating the projects, when we were reading through the European policies and regulations that ensured safety for the users and their sensitive data collected via the system. It is also efficient to collect ideas about how to display the project collections, how to link in an effective way the submitters with the organisations, the accepted project proposals with the university students or how to map between projects and reviewers. Their easiness and smoothness in implementing these flows and rendering the otherwise complicate back-end steps in such a skilfull manner led towards us brainstorming and coming up with useful suggestions for our clients.

Learning points from the existing platforms


  • Providing the option to utilise templates greatly reduces the difficult and time in creating proposals
  • User interfaces, specifically dashboards, must be easy to use, and presented in a clear and concise manner
  • Notifications should be sent to relevant parties in the event of any new action (signing, approval, rejection etc. )
  • Ability to electronically sign and pay should be an option
  • Tracking of signatures and analytics would be a good addition but not necessary to the functionality of the platform
  • Automated responses should be implemented once the platform grows in size

Technology Review


Possible Solutions

  • We could potentially have implemented a solution to our project using Workzone's project management software. Their system ensures secure file sharing, approvals workflow, comment features and task sharing. [4]

  • Another option was to use the basic but very powerful web development tool, Wix. It provides document storage and focuses on the web development space, providing us with freedom to design the page exactly as desired. In addition to this, a team can collaborate on building the site.[5]

  • Glasscubes, which markets itself as a SharePoint alternative, is a powerful project management software. It differentiates itself by claiming to focus on simplicity in both setup and usability. It provides a broad range of collaboration features, such as file sharing, project management, customisable workspaces and more.[6]

  • Confluence is a collaboration software that aims to create a centralised workspace. Its focus is on providing users with a large collection of templates that allow users to being working quickly and efficiently. It is also worth noting that Confluence can be combine with its sister product, Jira Software, a software development tool that helps teams plan, track and report on work. [7]

Possible Web Development Tools

  • Flask, a micro web framework written in Python, can be used to build a web application. Pre-existing third party libraries provide the developer with the ability to implement a complex and dynamic website.[8]

  • Web2py is a free open source full-stack framework for rapid dedvelopment of fast, scalable, secure and portable databse-driven web-based applications. It is compatible with most database systems and easy to set up.[9]

  • Angular, a TypeScript-based open source web application framework allows users to build apps for web, mobile web, native mobile and native desktop. Its features which will be useful for our system include templating, RESTful API handling and two-way binding. [10]

  • ReactJS, arguably the most popular Javascript application framework, allows users to create large web applications. These dynamic pages, can change data without reloading the page. [11]


Our choices

Microsoft Sharepoint
Sharepoint was chosen as the solution of choice due to its integration with the Microsoft environment. After conducting several meetings with our clients, it was evident that their systems along with UCL's relied on Microsoft. Because of this, it was the perfect choice, as our finished product would be able to seamlessly integrate with our clients and the deployment of our solution would not cause any issues. Furthermore, as this project would be in collaboration with the UCL IXN Programme, our system would be ideal for the main users of our system, the UCL IXN team, whose environment mainly relies on Microsoft systems.

Django
Django was chosen as the framework of choice for the public version of our Portal Engine due to influence from our clients, similar to Microsoft Sharepoint. Although there were many other options as outlined above in the "Possible Web Development Tools", we felt that, with agreement from our NHS client, Django provided us with all the necessary tools and technologies required to build our dynamic and complex database-driven web application. Furthermore, it was useful that this implementation was in Python, as it was a language we felt comfortable with, having went through a module in the first-year of our degree which focused on this programming language. After comparing the key features of Python with a wider gamut of programming languages, we decided this is the language to go. We took into account that despite the fact that Python is dynamically typed and required more testing due to potential runtime errors, it comes with extensive libraries and modules that covered our needs in web development. However, we encountered some initial hurdles when using Django because we needed to learn it from scratch. The process of selecting tutorials, of self-teaching, and of putting the theory into practice was thought-provoking and, indeed, challenging.


Summary of Technical Decisions

After careful consideration, our team decided to develop two separate implementations that provide the same functionalities. Due to the nature of requirements posed by our clients, we deemed it best to provide two platforms that are able to carry out all necessary features as outlined in the requirements page. Using the learning points stated above, we have made the following technical decisions.

  • Include a dashboard page that outlines all projects (accepted, submitted, history)
  • Ensure user interface is as simple and clear as possible
  • Automate notifications and responses when a proposal has been submitted, approved or completed
  • Use a form based submission system instead of a design/editor page for ease of use
  • Allow submitters to request for feedback and approval on their proposal page
  • Allow approvers to review feedback provided and decide on a accept/reject decision
